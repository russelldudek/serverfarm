## Visible company identity
The campaign uses a clearly typeset nominative `serverfarm` identifier above the fold, immediately paired with `Candidate vision for Data Center Campus Director - by Russell Dudek` and an independent-work disclaimer.

## Official logo decision
A usable official logo is publicly served by Serverfarm. The connected execution environment could identify the official asset URL but could not retrieve the binary through its network boundary, and the repository's existing automated acquisition workflow did not materialize it on `main`. The campaign therefore does **not** trace, redraw, recolor or fabricate the official mark. It uses a typeset company name pending direct binary acquisition. This is a documented technical limitation, not a claim that no official logo exists.

## Color token provenance
- `#a01c20` - source-sampled from Serverfarm's public red identity treatment.
- `#222426`, `#50555a`, `#d6d7d8`, `#ffffff` - source-sampled / structurally matched neutral family from the public site and logo treatment.
- `#f2b544`, `#4f8f72`, `#b45f3c` - candidate-original state colors for energized, controlled-ready and hold conditions. They remain subordinate to Serverfarm red/charcoal.

## Typography evidence and decision
Serverfarm's public site presents a modern geometric/technical sans-serif character with dense bold display headings, restrained body typography and strong black/red contrast. No authoritative public font specification or safely reusable proprietary font binary was located. The campaign uses the licensed local `Noto Sans Display` / `Noto Sans` family as a structural substitute; it is not described as Serverfarm's official font.

## Photography and environmental cues
Official pages emphasize large-scale campus exteriors, industrial infrastructure, high-density power/cooling, modular geometry, red/black interface accents and precise systems language. Because image-binary retrieval was unavailable, the campaign uses original one-line electrical/campus graphics rather than generic stock photography or copied internal interfaces.

## Role-derived visual grammar
- one-line electrical diagram / paralleling-board composition;
- heavy charcoal operational surfaces with Serverfarm red as the recognition layer;
- restrained technical rules, bus bars, breakers and status states;
- motion that energizes a complete path only when operating interlocks are resolved.

## Artifact application
- Website: fullest dark industrial expression and interactive Campus Paralleling Board.
- Resume / cover letter: white executive documents with restrained red rule and technical typography.
- Brief / plan / review: consulting-report structure using the same red, charcoal, rules and interlock vocabulary.

## Independent-candidate distinction
All surfaces identify the work as an independent candidate vision by Russell Dudek and state that it is not affiliated with or endorsed by Serverfarm.
