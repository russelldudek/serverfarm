Entry principle

Read the live system before changing it.

The first 120 days should protect existing expertise, reveal the actual risk-and-work system, install only the minimum useful operating mechanisms and earn the right to scale them.

North star: parallel growth with reliability - never transfer expansion pressure into hidden operating risk.

Outcomes by day 120

  • trusted stakeholder and authority map
  • baseline of recurring risk, work aging and customer friction
  • one bounded Campus Paralleling Review pilot
  • evidence that the mechanism improves decisions without adding low-value bureaucracy
  • selective standardization and a clear stop list

Non-goals

  • do not arrive with a generic transformation program
  • do not override technical or life-safety authority
  • do not treat dashboard activity as operational control
  • do not invent targets before baselines
  • do not standardize campus differences that matter

Days 0-30

Read and protect.

Listen
Conduct structured listening and floor-level observation.
Operators, engineering, facilities, security, customer care, finance, procurement, development/commissioning, vendors and customers. Ask what is working, what repeatedly surprises the campus and what current process protects.
Walk
Trace critical operating and decision paths.
Observe shift handoffs, alarm review, planned work, vendor entry, MOP/EOP review, customer communication, escalation and post-event learning under the authority of experienced site leaders.
Baseline
Create a source-grounded operating baseline.
Measurement categories: safety/near-miss learning, work-order aging, recurring alarms, corrective/preventive maintenance, customer escalations, vendor performance, procedure exceptions, commissioning readiness, OPEX/CAPEX visibility and PUE/WUE.
Map
Document decision rights and dependencies.
Which decisions remain local? Which require regional or executive authority? Where do construction, operations, customers and vendors exchange risk?

Deliverables

  • stakeholder / authority map
  • critical-path observations
  • baseline and data-quality register
  • top recurring operating tensions
  • explicit do-not-disrupt list

Exit criteria

  • site leaders recognize their reality in the diagnosis
  • no major recommendation rests on a single data source
  • authority boundaries are explicit
  • one bounded pilot opportunity is jointly selected
Leadership behavior: show respect through curiosity, not performative certainty.

Days 31-90

Stabilize, then prove.

31-45
Make recurring risk and ownership visible.
Separate one-off events from patterns. Clarify owners, due dates, evidence and escalation thresholds for repeat alarms, aging work, vendor misses and customer-impacting dependencies.
46-60
Design the minimum viable Paralleling Review.
Use one existing workflow; add only the five interlock questions and decision state. Define the baseline, participants, authority and stop conditions. Avoid a parallel bureaucracy.
61-75
Pilot on one bounded scenario.
Examples for discovery: planned maintenance, repeat alarm, vendor-dependent corrective work or operational handover. AI may synthesize evidence gaps, but human authority decides.
76-90
Review evidence and friction.
Did decision latency improve? Was ownership clearer? Did the pilot reduce repeat work or communication gaps? What burden did it add? What should be removed?

Days 91-120

Scale selectively.

Standardize only proven mechanisms

  • retain the interlocks that changed decisions
  • remove fields and meetings that added burden
  • document where campus variation is legitimate
  • assign durable owners and review cadence

Connect growth to operational acceptance

  • training and qualification evidence
  • MOP/EOP and contingency readiness
  • spares, vendor and service ownership
  • customer communication and escalation
  • commissioning punch-list and learning closure

Build a portfolio learning packet

  • what condition triggered the review
  • what evidence changed the decision
  • what remained local
  • what became repeatable
  • what should not be copied elsewhere

Present the 12-month operating agenda

  • top reliability mechanisms
  • people/capability priorities
  • vendor and customer trust priorities
  • economic and sustainability measures
  • growth-readiness roadmap
