Data Center Campus Director

Serverfarm's public growth story makes the Data Center Campus Director mandate unusually clear: accelerate capacity while keeping life safety, uptime, customer trust, financial discipline and operating standards intact across a live, increasingly complex portfolio.

My background is adjacent rather than conventional, and I would not disguise that. I do not claim a decade inside colocation or hyperscale data centers. I do bring the operating mechanics behind mission-critical leadership: 24/7 site cadence and customer-backward execution at Amazon; engineering, manufacturing, quality systems and root-cause discipline at Compunetics; regulated authority and a verified safety record through ZeusVu; and current cross-functional work connecting factory, field, customer and machine-fleet signals at Vape-Jet.

My candidate thesis is to parallel growth with reliability. Before new capacity, maintenance or change is allowed to transfer risk into the live campus, five interlocks should be explicit: authority, plant condition, procedure, partner readiness and customer continuity. The model is not a substitute for Serverfarm's existing systems or experienced technical leaders. It is a proposed review mechanism for turning visibility into an owned decision, bounded execution and reusable learning.

The independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/serverfarm/ demonstrates how I approach the role through a Campus Paralleling Board, balanced scorecard, hard-objection analysis and 120-day entry plan. It is intended to make my reasoning inspectable before an interview, not to imply knowledge of undisclosed Serverfarm conditions.

I would welcome the opportunity to test this thesis against Serverfarm's actual campus realities: where growth is creating the greatest handoff burden, which decisions should become more repeatable, and where local technical judgment must remain decisive.
